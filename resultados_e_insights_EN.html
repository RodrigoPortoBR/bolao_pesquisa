Research Report & Insights — CazéTV Bolão

Based on results from 15 questions applied via YouTube Lives to nearly 3,000 users per poll. Findings consolidated to guide the LiveLike MVP development.

🚀

1. Adoption Driver: Prizes Are the Hook

The main entry point for the average YouTube viewer into the predictor game is the promise of prizes.

61% say they would join the free predictor immediately if there were prizes. 41% say it is the top reason to play.

Product / Sales takeaway: The search for a Sponsor (iFood or Bets) who can supply these prizes is not a luxury — it is a strong necessity for driving adoption in Brazil.
🤝

2. Retention & Habit: Friend Leagues ("Resenha")

If prizes attract users, friends retain them.

When asked what makes the predictor game a habit, 49% chose "competing with friends." 52% would create a league just for friends, and 37% prefer that rivalry over the global ranking (13%).

MVP insight: Private Leagues (with a frictionless creation flow) must be prioritized over the Global Leaderboard in the LiveLike MVP. Without friends, engagement drops immediately after sign-up. Quick-share mechanics must be seamless.
🎙️

3. The "Fancentric" Factor: CazéTV Creators in Leagues

Blending the creator community with the fan experience unlocks a unique social layer.

59% think being able to add CazéTV hosts to their personal leagues would be "huge." 47% would like to see their ranking or bolão standings integrated directly into CazéTV's live stream.

Insight: The audience doesn't want a "silent second screen" — they want the predictor to interact with the live broadcast. LiveLike will need to provide clear APIs so the stream production team can pull the live Top 10 onto the YouTube screen in real time.
⏱️

4. Traffic Load Behavior: Server "Meltdown" Risk

This fully validates the infrastructure discussion from the technical kick-off:

40% will make their predictions minutes before kick-off, and another 34% on match day. Only 26% would submit well in advance.

Critical conclusion: CazéTV Bolão traffic will not be linear. The infrastructure (SSO / Database and the LiveLike app) will face a natural DDoS-like event — a massive spike of 5 to 8 million connections within a 15-minute window before kick-off. This reinforces the highest technical priority requirement for all third-party vendors.
📲

5. Notifications & Social Triggers

Users respond strongly to social hierarchy cues:

The most popular notification (44%) is "You moved up in the rankings!" Sharing results publicly is also strong: 36% would always post their results as Instagram Stories.

Final insight: The app must offer a fast, native "Export Card (Meme) to Instagram / WhatsApp" mechanism to fuel virality and attract the audience that wants to show off their predictions to the world.
